## Key Findings

### Week 1
- R&D dominates headcount at 67% — product-driven org profile
- 1 in 6 workers is a contractor (16.6%)
- Contractor pay premium: 64% more than Full-time equivalents in comparable roles
- Education vs pay paradox: Masters degree holders earn less than Bachelors

### Week 2
- Overtime is the #1 attrition driver: OT employees leave at 30.5% vs 10.4% for non-OT (3x multiplier)
- Burnout zone: OT + Good/Low performance rating = 60% attrition rate
- Job Involvement beats Job Satisfaction as a predictor: Low involvement → 33.7% exit rate
- HR department is the most at-risk segment: 45.5% attrition when Job Satisfaction is Low
- Reward structure is flat: Good, Low, and Excellent performers all receive ~14% hike — only Outstanding gets differentiated pay (21.8%)
- Manager relationship is a leading indicator: employees who left averaged 2.85 yrs with manager vs 4.37 for stayers
